Core Problem
AI agents need persistent, queryable knowledge about who I am to be useful. That knowledge has to come from somewhere. Journaling is how I generate that data.
What This Is
A voice journaling app that captures my thoughts, feelings, decisions, and experiences through natural conversation. The journal entries become the raw material for building a knowledge graph about me that AI can query.
Long-Term Goal
Every conversation I have with this app adds to a growing database of my thoughts and experiences. Over time, this becomes a comprehensive record that AI systems can use to understand:

My preferences across different contexts
My decision-making patterns and reasoning
My relationships and how I think about people
My emotional patterns and responses
My goals and how they've evolved

Why Voice
I hate writing. I love talking through my thoughts when someone asks good questions. Voice is the only way I'll actually journal consistently.
The Memory Loop

I talk about my day/thoughts/feelings
AI asks follow-up questions to extract detail
Conversation gets transcribed and stored
Key insights get synthesized into structured memory
Future conversations reference relevant past entries
This makes each session richer and more useful

The better the memory system, the better the questions, the better the data I generate.
Success Criteria
This works if:

I use it consistently (3+ times per week minimum)
The AI surfaces relevant past context naturally during conversations
The journal captures enough detail to be genuinely useful for future AI
The data is structured and portable

Non-Goals
This is NOT:

A mood tracker with charts
A productivity system
A therapy replacement
A social platform

If it doesn't improve the journaling experience or the quality of memory, it's scope creep.
